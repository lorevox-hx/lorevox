# Contributing to Lorevox

Lorevox is **source-available proprietary software**. Contributions are accepted by **invitation only**.

Do not submit pull requests, patches, code, prompts, documentation, schemas, assets, training data, model outputs, or design materials unless you have been explicitly invited to contribute. Pull requests submitted without prior invitation and a signed Contributor Assignment Agreement may be closed without review.

If you are interested in contributing, the right first step is to open a GitHub issue describing what you would like to work on, or email **dev@lorevox.com**. The copyright holder will respond with whether the proposed work fits the project, and if so, will send the Contributor Assignment Agreement for signature before any code is shared.

---

## Why this is different from most open-source projects

Lorevox is not under an OSI-approved open-source license. It is published source-available so that researchers, operators, older-adult-care professionals, and family-history practitioners can read, study, and run the software locally — but the code, prompts, persona, schemas, and architecture are reserved by the copyright holder. The same goes for the Lori interviewer character, the runtime71 cognitive state system, the multi-pass interview model, the four-layer family-truth pipeline, and the Cognitive Support Model.

Because Lorevox may eventually be commercialized, separated into companion products, or relicensed, every contribution needs a clear chain of rights from the contributor to the copyright holder. The Lorevox Contributor Assignment Agreement is how that gets done.

## What you must NOT submit

Even after invitation and a signed agreement, please do **not** submit:

- Code, text, prompts, assets, or data copied from another project unless clearly identified, attributed, and legally compatible with the Lorevox License
- Code from your employer, school, or any other party unless you have written permission to contribute it under the Contributor Assignment Agreement
- Private family data, narrator data, transcripts, photos, audio, memoir drafts, or other personally-identifying content from yourself or anyone else
- Confidential information of any kind
- Material that depends on a license incompatible with the Lorevox Source-Available Proprietary License (e.g. strong-copyleft licenses such as AGPL when applied to the codebase as a whole)

If you are not sure whether something is appropriate to submit, ask first.

## Contributor Assignment Agreement (required)

Before any contribution is accepted, the contributor must sign the **Lorevox Contributor Assignment Agreement** (template at `docs/contributor-assignment-agreement.md` in this repository) or another written agreement approved by the copyright holder.

By signing the agreement and contributing, you confirm that:

- The contribution is your original work, or you have the legal right to submit it
- The contribution does not include code, text, prompts, assets, data, or other materials copied from another project unless clearly identified and legally compatible
- The contribution does not include private family data, narrator data, transcripts, photos, memoir drafts, or confidential information
- You assign to the Lorevox copyright holder all rights, title, and interest in the contribution, with the broadest reach permitted by law
- The copyright holder may use, modify, sublicense, commercialize, and relicense the contribution under any terms, including proprietary or commercial terms, without further obligation to you
- You receive no ownership of Lorevox, the Lori interviewer persona, the interview system, the memoir pipeline, the prompts, the brand, or any Lorevox-specific architecture in exchange for your contribution

## Process at a glance

1. **Open an issue or email dev@lorevox.com** describing the work you would like to do. Wait for an explicit invitation before sending code.
2. **Sign the Contributor Assignment Agreement** that the copyright holder sends you. Return the signed copy by email.
3. **Submit your contribution as a pull request.** Direct push access is not granted. Every contribution flows through PR review.
4. **Review and possible revision.** The copyright holder may ask for changes, may decline to merge, or may merge with modifications. Acceptance of any contribution is at sole discretion.
5. **Acceptance is contingent on the signed agreement.** A merged PR that turns out not to be backed by a signed agreement may be reverted.

## What kinds of contributions are most welcome

The areas where outside collaboration makes the most sense at this stage are:

- **Research and evaluation.** Writing about Lorevox, evaluating it against benchmarks, applying it (with permission) in academic studies of older-adult human–AI interaction, life review, or memoir generation.
- **Bug reports backed by reproductions.** Detailed bug reports with steps to reproduce, even without code, are valuable.
- **Documentation improvements.** Clarifications, corrections, and improved onboarding for operators and researchers.
- **Subject-matter expertise.** Geriatric medicine, occupational therapy, dementia care, hospice care, family history practice, or trauma-informed interviewing — domains where Lorevox is operating but the copyright holder is not the deepest expert.

Areas that are **NOT open for outside contribution** at this stage:

- The Lori interviewer persona, voice design, or behavioral directives
- The system prompt content or prompt composer pipeline
- The memoir schema, family-truth pipeline, or biographical data structures
- The Cognitive Support Model, cognitive mode intelligence, or affect pipeline
- The narrator templates that ship with the codebase
- Branding, naming, or marketing materials

If you have ideas for any of those areas, please open an issue or email — the copyright holder is happy to discuss but is not currently accepting code in those parts of the system.
