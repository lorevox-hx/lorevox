**[LORI]:** Family life at home?

**[MARIA]:** Bilingual from the beginning — I insisted. Spanish at home, English at school. My mother lived with us for ten years and she spoke only Spanish so the children had no choice. We are a loud family. Loud and close.

**[LORI]:** Community involvement?

## Cross-Subject Strengths

**1. The question set is solid**
All four subjects — across age, gender, background, and memory quality — were able to engage with all 30 questions. No question was truly unanswerable. The section structure (Early Years → Adolescence → Career → Later Years) creates a natural arc.

**2. The format handles different answer lengths well**
Bob's terse three-word answers and Maria's multi-paragraph narratives both stored cleanly. The JSONL format is length-agnostic. This is a genuine architectural strength.

**3. Memory gaps don't break anything**
Dorothy's "I can't bring up her last name right now" and Bob's "I barely knew" are both stored as-is with no pressure to resolve them. The `is_approximate` and confidence fields in the facts table handle this cleanly.

**4. Rich emotional content emerges naturally**
Maria's Abuela Rosa, Bob's unspoken grief for Shirley, Jim's complex feelings about his father, Dorothy's three wasted years over a forgotten argument — none of these were forced. The question structure creates the conditions for them to surface.

**5. The two-process design holds**
LLM and TTS as separate processes on port 8000/8001 means TTS latency never blocks the interview engine. Critical for a live subject who is waiting for Lori to ask the next question.

---

## Cross-Subject Weaknesses Confirmed

**1. Short-answer subjects stress the section summary**
Bob's terse answers give the section summary LLM very little to work with. A section summary after "Do the work. Don't make excuses." is going to be thin. Minimum content threshold needed.

**2. Complex family timelines need better calendar support**
Jim has two sequential marriages and children from the second. The calendar currently creates timeline events per marriage but has no concept of "marriage ended" linked to "marriage began." The `end_date` field handles the range but the UI doesn't display it yet.

**3. No voice input pipeline tested**
All four simulations were text-only. The live subject experience is still untested. Whisper STT with p335 TTS read-back needs a real session before any of these strengths or weaknesses can be validated in real time.

**4. Spanish proper nouns are a Whisper risk**
Maria would say "Guelaguetza", "escuela primaria", "Conchita", "abuela". Whisper large-v3 handles Spanish well but mixed-language utterances mid-sentence are a known weak spot. A post-processing review flag is needed.

**5. Memoir output not tested**
None of the four simulations produced a full memoir because the `draft_final_memoir()` function targets 500–900 words. A 55-year-old and an 86-year-old deserve different depth. The chapter structure needs to scale to the subject's life complexity.
